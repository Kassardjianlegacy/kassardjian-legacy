Aksor Kassardjian – The Founding Pillar (1930s–1970s)

In the 1930s, during the British era of the Gold Coast of Africa, Aksor Kassardjian began building the foundation of what would become one of the most influential family legacies in Ghana. He developed deep networks with fellow merchant elites including the Kalenderians and the Kalmouni family, entering business with trust and strength.

Following Ghana’s independence in 1957, Aksor was invited to state ceremonies during the visit of Queen Elizabeth II and the King of the United Kingdom, recognized as a top national contractor. The royal family had already known and respected him since the early 1950s.

Through Kassardjian Construction Ghana Ltd., Aksor led the construction of Ghana’s core infrastructure including highways from Tamale to Accra, Kumasi to Tamale, and back to Accra. He held exclusive rights to supply JCB and Caterpillar (CAT) machinery, delivering directly to government projects.

He was revered by Ghanaian presidents from Dr. Kwame Nkrumah onward, often called upon personally for government-level deals. No one dared to challenge Aksor’s integrity, reach, or efficiency.

Beyond business, Aksor founded the Kassardjian School in Tamale, offering free education to children in need. Known for his generous heart, he frequently supported ministries with equipment and aid—always for the betterment of national service.
